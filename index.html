I am a fourth year PhD student at the University of Toronto visiting at MIT advised by Marzyeh Ghassemi. I am interested in learning robust representations of discrete-valued sequence data, as well as developing methods to characterize and fix their pathologies. My research covers questions in language modeling, denoising, and discrete representations, as well as OOD detection, generalization, and fairness.

Previously I have spent summers interning at Prescient Design with Kyunghyun Cho, Meta with Naman Goyal, and Google with Narendran Thangarajan. I also spent a year working at Facebook AI Research advised by Michael Auli. I completed my undegraduate degree in Computer Science with minors in Mathematics and Music at UCSD in 2018 advised by Julian McAuley and Zachary Lipton.
