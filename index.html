about me   news   publications

I am a fourth year PhD student at the University of Toronto visiting at MIT advised by Marzyeh Ghassemi. My research focuses on learning robust representations by leveraging the lower-dimensional manifold structure of data. Currently I am especially interested in applying these methods to complex discrete-valued data such as natural language and -omics data to improve out-of-domain robustness and detection.

Previously I completed my undegraduate degree in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics at UCSD in 2018 advised by Julian McAuley and Zachary Lipton. I also spent a year working at Facebook AI Research advised by Michael Auli.

conference publications

Predicting Out-of-Domain Generalization with Local Manifold Smoothness
Nathan Ng, Neha Hulkund, Kyunghyun Cho, Marzyeh Ghassemi
Submitted to TMLR

We propose a complexity measure based on measuring the smoothness of a classifier in a manifold neighborhood around a given test point and demonstrate its correlation with out-of-domain generalization on a large suite of models and tasks.

If Influence Functions are the Answer, Then What is the Question?
Juhan Bae, Nathan Ng, Alston Lo, Marzyeh Ghassemi, Roger Grosse
NeurIPS 2022

We decompose the sources of error that cause misalignment between influence function estimates and the results of true leave-one-out retraining in neural networks. Our results show that influence function estimates are a good approximation for a different object we call the proximal Bregman response function (PBRF) that can still be used to answer many of the questions that originally motivated influence functions.

SSMBA: Self-Supervised Manifold Based Data Augmentation for Improving Out-of-Domain Robustness
Nathan Ng, Kyunghyun Cho, Marzyeh Ghassemi
EMNLP 2020

We propose SSMBA, a data augmentation method based on moving along the data manifold by corrupting and reconstructing examples with a denoising autoencoder. We investigate SSMBA on a large array of natural language tasks and find it significantly improves both in-domain and out-of-domain generalization compared to baseline methods.

Improving Dialogue Breakdown Detection with Semi-Supervised Learning Nathan Ng, Marzyeh Ghassemi, Narendran Thangarajan, Jiacheng Pan, Qi Guo
International Workshop on Spoken Dialogue Systems Technology 2020

We investigate the use of semi-supervised learning techniques for the task of dialogue breakdown detection, including continued pre-training and data augmentation. Our final model placed first in the DBDC5 2020 shared task.
